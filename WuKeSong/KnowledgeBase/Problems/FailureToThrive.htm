Failure to thrive

Last updated: Tuesday, 30, November, 2010
Causes Appropriate Tests

Clinical assessment, including measurement of height and weight, assessment of growth over time.

Urine - microscopy and culture

Reduced nutrient availability

Dietary deficiency

Vomiting

Diarrhoea, including

  • Malabsorption, especially
    • Cystic fibrosis
    • Coeliac disease
    • Food intolerance

See under Diarrhoea.

Intestinal infection

Increased nutrient requirements

Infection, especially

  • Urinary tract infection
  • Respiratory infection
  • Giardiasis

If not apparent, consider possibility of occult infection: FBC, differential WCC, blood film, ESR or CRP may be helpful.

See under Cough - chronic.

Immunodeficiency, especially

  • AIDS

Inborn errors of metabolism

Rare causes, consider if above excluded; consult pathologist

Glossary Terms

AIDS:

Acquired immunodeficiency syndrome

CRP :

C-reactive protein

ESR :

Erythrocyte sedimentation rate

FBC :

Full blood count , full blood examination, complete blood count

WCC :

white cell count, leucocyte count
